The Game That Refused to Give Up

No Man's Sky is a testament to developer love. It is a universe that feels infinite because it is, and yet it feels personal because of the traces we leave behind.

Hello Games
Exploration / Survival
PC / PS5 / Xbox
~15 min read

I was first introduced to No Man's Sky in 2019, and I instantly fell in love with it, even if I wasn’t that good at it. Dying first on a radioactive planet, then a frosty one—it was a trial by fire, but the universe kept pulling me back.

The scale of No Man's Sky is difficult to comprehend until you're staring at the horizon of a world no one else has ever seen.

But there was something always so amazing about No Man's Sky to me. It is a place where I find creatures which were never discovered before, I find planets no one ever knew existed, and yet, I find discovered planets too—places people have been, bases people have left years prior, and remain forever. Remnants of someone once forever changes this universe.

The game has received nothing but constant love and updates from the developers, and I have to break my own oath for this review.

"I play every game to completion before writing a single word. If I haven't finished it, you won't find a review of it here."

I can't say I have played this game to completion, because every time I spend a hundred or so hours exploring new content, the devs immediately release more content and make me have to restart this review.

The Most Content for its Price

No Man's Sky is the game that I will say has the most content for its price. It features:

  • 3 entire languages to learn
  • A plethora of secrets and lore
    "The universe is 1 of 6 possibilities..."
  • The Atlas, and Nexus, both offering characters and things to do
  • Thousands of thousands of ships to find and explore
  • Storylines on abandoned planets
  • Deep Sea Horrors that scared the life out of me
  • Broken down space stations and giant floating wrecks filled with horrific horrors
Wormy :)

Overall, the game offers so many playstyles so I would recommend this game to anyone.

PVE as pirates raiding peaceful and friendly ships, being attacked by giant ships
Farm and Build and live a peaceful life on a desolate planet with no one in many light years to bat an eye
Find whatever this guy is (Seriously, what is he?)

The thing is, I could list many more, but I won't, it will be outdated in 3 months anyways. Recently they added modular ships you can sky dive from or walk on the inside of, or even float around in space from, and then right as I started this review, they added battling with animals—you can basically play Pokemon with procedurally generated animals. Some may have an advantage, for example, Wormy :).

This game has no ending, and is not a hard game. As soon as you hit lift off on a new save file, death is fairly rare—at least punishable death. For those of you playing perma-death, you are brave; I fell through my freighter one too many times to do that.

I will not say this game is perfect, but I will say, this game has some of the most love out of any game I have ever seen, or even heard of. This game went from a wreck on launch to managing multiple cities, planets, universes, creatures, ships, fleets.

Rosie Approved

If any game deserves the Rosie Approved stamp, even if this game does not have my highest rating, this game earns it.
